Gait Biometrics

Cross-view gait recognition on CASIA-B.

GitHub

Technologies

PyTorchOpenCVCNN CASIA-BTransfer Learning

Detailed explanation

  • Problem: Traditional biometric systems fail when faces are obscured or captured from a distance. Gait recognition provides a non-intrusive alternative by identifying individuals via walking patterns, but introduces extreme complexities regarding cross-view camera angles and clothing variations.
  • Approach: For this long-term research initiative, I architected a deep learning pipeline to process silhouette sequences from the CASIA-B dataset. I utilized custom CNNs and transfer learning (Xception) to extract robust spatial features, which were then fed into an LSTM network to model the temporal dynamics of the human walk.
  • Results & Status: The transfer learning architecture achieved a peak accuracy of 98%, demonstrating strong capability in handling both same-view and cross-view challenges. This is an active, ongoing team project where we are currently focused on improving view-invariant representations and metric learning.
  • Learnings: Building this system pushed my deep learning skills beyond static image classification and into complex temporal sequence modeling (CNN +Transfer Learning). It taught me the critical importance of rigorous evaluation design—specifically, how to structure training splits to prevent data leakage across different camera angles.

Customer Churn Prediction

Explainable churn classifier with SHAP.

GitHub

Key results

  • AUC-ROC: 0.95, Accuracy: 93.2%.
  • Precision (Churn): 0.91, Recall (Churn): 0.89, F1: 0.90.
  • Explainability: SHAP used to interpret drivers (contract type, monthly charges).

Technologies

Scikit-learnXGBoostSHAPPlotly

Detailed explanation

  • Problem: Customer churn is a critical issue for subscription-based businesses, and accurately predicting which customers are likely to leave can enable targeted retention strategies. However, many predictive models lack interpretability, making it difficult for business stakeholders to understand the key drivers of churn.
  • Approach: I developed a robust classification pipeline to predict customer churn using a combination of data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model optimization. The pipeline included handling class imbalance with techniques like SMOTE and evaluating multiple algorithms (Random Forest, XGBoost) to identify the best-performing model. To enhance interpretability, I integrated SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) to provide clear insights into which features were most influential in driving churn predictions.
  • Results & Deployment: The final XGBoost model achieved an AUC-ROC of 0.95 and an accuracy of 93.2%. The SHAP analysis revealed that contract type and monthly charges were the most significant factors influencing churn, providing actionable insights for business decision-makers. The project is designed for easy integration into customer relationship management (CRM) systems to facilitate real-time churn prediction and intervention.
  • Learnings: This project reinforced the importance of model interpretability in machine learning applications, especially in business contexts where understanding the "why" behind predictions is crucial for stakeholder buy-in. It also highlighted the value of rigorous evaluation metrics beyond just accuracy when dealing with imbalanced classification problems.
